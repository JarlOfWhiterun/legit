Geema

I start off with the best of the best, I might be a little biased when it comes to Geema(you know us southerners love our Geema). In my eyes it holds the title of "Thee delicacy". Have a plate.

Timan wa Marag Bahmya(Rice n'Okra soup)

With this one it was always a love hate relationship. I love it and you will too, but my mother always used to make it and it kinda made me dislike growing up, but now i dont take it for granted.

Tepsi

Tepsi holds a warm place in my( all these foods are my own soul foods fyi),the mixes of fried vegetables are so delectable...ahhh its so mouthwatering.

Kabob

Many people are probably wondering why this isnt number one, good question because honestly I don't know either. Their is a difference between your kabob and our Kabob, Our kabob(iraqi) actually tastes good. hehehe

Ragee

Ragee is watermelon, I.Love.Watermelon

Sim wa Marath(posion and illness)

This was what my mom told us to eat when she was mad at us and we were hungry....I love you mama.

Dolma

Dolma is famous mostly across the meditarreantian but iraqis really go hard with this dish, mananging to mix both sweet and sour flavors to make an expectional "Dolma" dish

kebbah

Another food that isnt just iraqi, but in my opinon is best perfected by the iraqis is the meaty/veggie delicacy we call 'Kebbah', Iraqi's tend to add a blend of spices when creating this dish.

Guss

This is for the boys back home. Seriously though Guss is so good. Guss literally means "cut". Its a piece of bread cut down the middle added into it is meat(of your liking)and veggies with the secret Guss sauce added to it.

Masgouf(fish)

This has to be the reason why Iraqi's are all strong. this right here. It's safe to say that I've never tasted a flavorless fish. Masgouf utterly destroys your taste buds with an astonishing and overwhelming flavor of spices...depending on who the cook is the fish can range from sweet to zesty to spicy.
